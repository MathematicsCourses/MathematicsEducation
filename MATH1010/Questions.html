  • (37.) Answer: The main purpose of the Initial Due Date and Final Due Date is to guard my students against dropping the class (retention) and to give them enough time to do their work to get good grades (success).
    In the Education profession (in the United States), two of the main evaluations (there are several) that affect a teacher are the: Retention Rate and the Success Rate.
    Retention rate: If several registered students drop the class, it does not speak well of the teacher.
    Success rate: If several registered students fail the class, it does not speak well of the teacher.

    Retention: I do not want my students to drop the class.
    Hence, I allow them time to complete their work past the initial due date without penalty (for major assessments besides the Discussions because Discussions are required weekly).
    If I put penalty or if I do not accept student work past the initial due date, then students will likely drop the class.
    That is the one of the reasons for having two due dates: to keep my students in the class and prevent them from dropping the class.
    Do you think I would still have a contract if at least 50% of my students drop the class especially if it is a continuing pattern?

    The unintended consequence: Procrastination.
    Students tend to procrastinate if they know that they still have the opportunity to complete it.
    That is where the initial due date plays a role. I go ahead and put in zeros as applicable for assessment not done past the initial due date to alert my students that this assessment has not been done, and needs to be done.
    Imagine if I gave only one due date. Some students will not do any work and just wait till the last minute and rush their work.
    Rushing their work will likely lead to errors which might make them to fail the class.

    Success: Students have diverse learning styles.
    Some students may take an hour to solve 20 questions and get them correctly.
    Some students may take 2 hours, 3 hours, 4 hours, or even 1 day, to solve the 20 questions and get them correctly.
    I want to accommodate all those students.
    Hence, I open up all the questions on MLM from the first day of class and allow everyone to work at their own pace provided they complete everything by the final due date.
    The final due date helps students who need more time to complete their work.
    For the project, some students can complete it in a day, some may complete it in two days, one week, etc.
    Hence, I also give them time to complete it by the final due date.
    The final due date is to help students who need more time to complete their work.

    So the two main reasons for having two due dates are: the Retention and the Success of my students.
